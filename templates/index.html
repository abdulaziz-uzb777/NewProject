Black Holes & Dark Matter

Black Holes

Regions of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape once it crosses the event horizon.

  • Formed when massive stars collapse
  • Can have millions of times the mass of our Sun
  • Time slows down near black holes due to gravity
  • They slowly evaporate through Hawking radiation

Dark Matter

Invisible matter that makes up about 27% of the universe. We can't see it directly, but we know it exists because of its gravitational effects.

  • Five times more abundant than regular matter
  • Doesn't emit, absorb, or reflect light
  • Helps hold galaxies together
  • Its true nature remains one of physics' greatest mysteries

Dark Energy

A mysterious force causing the universe's expansion to accelerate. It makes up about 68% of the universe.

  • Discovered through observations of distant supernovae
  • Acts opposite to gravity on cosmic scales
  • May determine the ultimate fate of the universe
  • Could lead to the "Big Rip" scenario
